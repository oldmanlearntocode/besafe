CASUAL ENCOUNTERS

South Africa is not a safe place!
Casual sexual encounters are certainly not recommended.
From a personal safety perspective it is extremely risky and dangerous.


If you are going to engage in casual encounters
make sure that:

  • you never meet up with more than one person at a time.
  • you get the cellphone number of the person that you are planning to meet.
  • you tell a friend that you are meeting another person for a casual encounter, and provide your friend with the person's contact information.
  • you make use of mobile tracker apps so that people can track you if neccessary.
  • should you decide to meet at your home make sure that you communicate this to someone who can check up on you regularly.
  • you do not leave valuable items lying around at your house.
  • you keep an escape route open at your house in case it is needed.
  • you do not engage in any kind of BDSM that can leave you vulnerable.
  • you take up some kind of self defence course.
  • you arm yourself with some kind of weapon or deterent like pepper spray.
  • if you feel uncomfortable remove yourself immediately from the situation.
  • If you are blackmailed or harrased immediately open a case at the SAPS and inform your local LGBTI+ organisations. There is an entire network of LGBTI+ organisations in South Africa that can assist.

Remember you alone are resposible for your personal safety!
Rather put your personal safety first and stay away from casual sexual encounters.
