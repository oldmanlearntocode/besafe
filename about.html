ABOUT

This website was created in
remembrance of all those LGTBI+ people that are no longer with us.

  • This is a not-for-profit initiative.
  • This website is essentially a reflection of crime incidents against LGBTI+ individuals that fell victim, not only to crime in general, but specifically to alleged or confirmed hate crimes as reported in the media over time.
  • Note that comprehensive hate crime legislation does not exist in South Africa. This makes the reporting and classification of crime against the LGBTI+ community extremely difficult.
  • This is a first attempt and it remains work in progress. Please feel free to provide us with constructive criticism and suggestions that might assist us to improve on this initiative.
  • It is our wish that this website and database will assist other researchers, journalists and bloggers when writing and reporting not only on crime in general but specifically Hate Crimes Against the LGBTI+ Community in South Africa.
  • This website also brings together a disparate group of academic and non-academic sources on crimes against the LGBTI+ Community in South Africa. Many more remain outstanding and we will continue to add more open-source material as time goes on.
  • We took great care not to infringe on any copyright laws and where possible extensively acknowledge and reference newspaper articles and blog posts of other journalists and reporters that regularly report on crimes against the LGBTI+ Community.
  • As a journalist or publisher if for any reason you feel that you have not been sufficiently acknowledged, or "miss quoted" in the source reports or you would like your information to be removed from this website please get into contact with us and we will gladly do so.
  • We also invested time in a list of safety guidelines for our LGBTI+ Community. The guidelines focus on personal safety when going out, visiting clubs and taverns, making use of dating apps as well as meeting up with potential friends. South Africa is not a safe country and it is important that we put our safety first.
  • On our side we will keep this project alive for as long as possible. The current state of crime but more specifically hate crimes against the LGBTI+ community in South Africa is of particular concern. When studying the media reports it is clear that there is a steady increase in crimes against the LGBTI+ community compared to previous years.
  • As source reports become available in the public domain we will update the site.
  • Please report any discrepancies to us. It is important that we remain as accurate as possible in our reporting.
  • The following remain outstanding: LGBTI+ Hate Crime Database. Whatsapp group for journalists. This will activate a larger footprint of journalists reporting on hate crimes in the media. A central whatsapp number for LGBTI+ community to report hate crimes.
